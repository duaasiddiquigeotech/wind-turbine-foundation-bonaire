# Wind-Turbine Foundation Design: Bonaire Case Study

## Overview

This repository presents a geotechnical and rock-mechanics assessment for wind-turbine foundation design in Bonaire.

The project combines desk study, geological interpretation, site-investigation planning, ground-model development, geohazard assessment, and evaluation of possible foundation concepts.

## Engineering Problem

Wind turbines apply significant vertical, horizontal, overturning, and cyclic loads to their foundations.

The foundation system must therefore be designed with a clear understanding of:

- site geology,
- rock-mass conditions,
- groundwater,
- geohazards,
- bearing capacity,
- settlement,
- sliding,
- overturning,
- and construction feasibility.

## Project Objectives

The main objectives were to:

- develop a geological and geotechnical understanding of the site,
- identify relevant geohazards,
- prepare a site-investigation strategy,
- interpret available geophysical and borehole information,
- construct a representative ground model,
- assess shallow and deep foundation options,
- and evaluate the suitability of foundation concepts for wind-turbine loading.

## Methodology

The project was completed through the following stages:

1. Desk study
2. Review of topography, climate, geology, and tectonic setting
3. Hydrological and geohazard assessment
4. Site-investigation planning
5. Geophysical interpretation
6. Borehole and trial-pit interpretation
7. Ground-model development
8. Rock-mass characterisation
9. Foundation-concept assessment
10. Preliminary design checks

## Site Characterisation

The site assessment considered:

- geomorphology,
- geological history,
- lithology,
- discontinuities,
- groundwater conditions,
- weathering,
- topography,
- and construction constraints.

## Site Investigation

The proposed investigation programme included:

- geological mapping,
- geophysical surveys,
- boreholes,
- trial pits,
- laboratory testing,
- rock-core logging,
- groundwater observations,
- and engineering classification of the subsurface.

## Ground Model

The ground model was developed from the combined interpretation of geological, geophysical, and geotechnical information.

It was used to define:

- engineering units,
- expected variability,
- strength and stiffness ranges,
- discontinuity conditions,
- groundwater assumptions,
- and potential foundation levels.

## Foundation Assessment

The project considered the suitability of different foundation systems, including:

- shallow gravity foundations,
- rock-supported foundations,
- anchored foundations,
- and deep foundation alternatives where required.

## Engineering Checks

The assessment included consideration of:

- bearing resistance,
- settlement,
- sliding stability,
- overturning stability,
- uplift,
- cyclic loading,
- global stability,
- and constructability.

## Limitations

The project was based on academic case-study data and therefore includes uncertainty related to:

- limited site-specific investigation,
- assumed material parameters,
- simplified loading conditions,
- geological variability,
- and preliminary design-level calculations.

The results should be interpreted as a concept-stage engineering assessment rather than a final foundation design.
